About

My Story

After graduating from the University of Denver with a Bachelor of Science in Biology and Psychology, I started at Summit Dermatology as a medical assistant. I aspired to gain medical experience so I could continue my career as a physician assistant. As time went on I started to realize that I liked medicine less and less. The redundancy, insurance games, lack of personal connection... I realized it was not for me.

I started taking on different projects within the office: writing a 100-paged training manual for the clinical staff, implementing new technologies (i.e. Kiosks for check-in, Telehealth, etc.), modernizing the practice, etc. I began to realize that I really loved improving on inefficient, outdated policies and practices throughout the clinic. Because of this desire to improve the practice, they promoted me to Project Manager. As a project manager I began to take on more and more responsibilities that coincided with understanding and implementing the user experience. Though my job title was not "UX Designer" I felt as though I was implementing several of the UX methodologies. This little taste of UX helped me realize the direction I wanted to take for my career. I signed up for an intensive project-based training program through CareerFoundry with a focus on UX Design, UX Research, UI Design, and Frontend Development.

At the time that I finished my course, my company was moving towards changing EMR systems and asked me to stay on to help see this project through. I was the front runner in customizing and designing our new EMR system to increase the usability and functionality for our specific practice. Now that the practice has transitioned over to the new EMR seamlessly, I am looking for a new position where I can work full time as a UX Designer or Researcher.
