My Story

After graduating from the University of Denver with a double major in Psychology and Biology, I started working as a medical assistant at a dermatology clinic to get some medical experience for my PA applications. The more time I spent working in the medical field the more I realized… I hated it. I started taking on more administrative tasks and I was eventually promoted to project manager because of my desire to constantly improve on the practice. My main responsibility was to upgrade the entire practice by implementing new technologies and policies. I could tell I was starting to head in the right direction but I still did not have my answer. After endless scrolling on Indeed, I finally came across a posting for a UX designer. I had never even heard of this position before but, after more research, I realized that this checked all of my boxes. I could solve problems creatively, apply my background of psychology, and have tangible results that can improve people’s lives. A week later, I was so certain that this is what I wanted to do that I signed up for a UX Design course with Career Foundry. I am now a proud creative problem solver and I have yet to look back!
